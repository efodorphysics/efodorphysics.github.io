Nonequilibrium soft and active matter

The aim of this course is to introduce how some recent methods of statistical Physics can be used to model the complex dynamics of soft and active systems. Active systems are made of interacting agents able to extract energy stored in the environment to produce directed motion. The local conversion of energy into mechanical work drives the system far from equilibrium, yielding dynamics and phases without any equivalent in passive systems. Using some minimal models of passive and active systems, this course illustrates how to distinguish equilibrium and nonequilibrium features of the dynamics, and how to rationalize the emergence of collective behaviors.
